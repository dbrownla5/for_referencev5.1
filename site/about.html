About

Operational support for modern life.

Most clients hire me because the operational side of life quietly became heavier than it used to be.

The work is rarely just about the object.

Modern life quietly creates hundreds of small unfinished decisions. The lamp nobody knows what to do with. The unit no one wants to open. The setup that never got finished. Individually, none of them are a big deal. Together, they're the reason a weekend disappears and nothing feels handled.

I'm the person who handles them. One capable point of contact for the logistics, the follow-through, the vendor coordination, the resale decisions, the weird lamp, the storage unit. All of it.

Competence does not eliminate the need to be cared for.

How I think about it

Grounded competence, not a sales pitch.

Some clients are busy

They don't want to spend the weekend assembling furniture, coordinating vendors, and managing resale platforms. Convenience is not a moral failure.

Some are in transition

A move, a downsize, an inherited home, a changed household. Being capable doesn't mean someone should have to carry everything alone.

All of it is valid

Support shouldn't require a crisis. People deserve a capable hand without being made to feel incapable.

How I work

Calm, practical, and almost invisible.

A lot of this happens between visits — routing, coordination, scheduling, resale prep, vendor follow-up. The business is built around trust-based, asynchronous workflows: a key handoff, a route pickup, a solo reset while you're at work or out of town.

Based in Los Angeles. Local travel is generally folded into the work and routed for efficiency — you're paying for the work and the follow-through, not for someone standing in your kitchen.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is movement.

For clarity

A few things this isn't.

This is practical operational support — not junk hauling, hoarding or biohazard remediation, a heavy-moving labor crew, legal estate administration, therapy, or family arbitration. Inherited-home and transition work covers the practical aftermath, not legal estate administration or deciding who gets what.
