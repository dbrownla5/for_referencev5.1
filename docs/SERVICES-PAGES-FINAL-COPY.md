# PAGE: HOME ORGANIZATION & MOVE SUPPORT

## Headline

**Home Resets & Move Support.**

Your home, made to work for how you actually live.

## Opening

Usually, it starts with one problem.

But most of the time, the hard part isn't actually the boxes.

It's dealing with everything tied to them while life keeps moving around you.

The move that's about way more than just moving.

The house everyone walks through one last time because every room still feels full of memories.

The garage packed with "just in case" stuff that meant something to someone you love.

The storage unit and resale pile holding onto pieces of who you used to be.

At some point, you realize you don't just need movers or organizers.

You need someone who gets that people move through memories at their own pace—someone who can keep things moving without pushing past grief, nostalgia, or indecision.

That's where I come in.

This is for the room that keeps collecting piles, the move that technically happened but never settled, the closet that no longer fits your life, and the spaces where everyday friction quietly steals time.

## Recognition

You'll know if this is you:

The nursery after the baby shower.

The move that technically happened six months ago.

The storage unit that started as temporary and quietly became part of the monthly budget for six years.

The closet that's worth dealing with but somehow never becomes today's priority.

The guest room that quietly became storage.

The garage that became the holding area for decisions nobody wanted to make.

The home office that became a catch-all.

The household functioning because everyone memorized the chaos.

The room that used to have a purpose.

The room that still could.

## Who This Is For

People who aren't lazy and aren't disorganized. People who are overloaded.

The new parents staring at a nursery's worth of gifts with nowhere to put them. The couple whose move went fine except for the eleven boxes nobody has opened since. The person who inherited furniture, kept the storage unit, and stopped looking at the bill. The family whose kitchen works only because everyone has memorized where the broken drawer sticks.

You don't need a system. You don't need an app. You need another capable person who can walk in, see the whole picture clearly, and start untangling what's actually stuck.

If you've ever typed "is there a person who can help me finish unpacking" or "is there a person who can help me figure out what to keep" — yes. There's a person. I'm Dayna.

## What This Covers

- Room-by-room resets — kitchens, closets, garages, offices, nurseries, the room nobody opens
- Unpacking and post-move settling, whether the move was last week or last year
- Closet editing — keep, sell, donate, decided with you, not for you
- Layout and setup so the space works for how your household actually runs
- Move staging, packing decisions, and coordination with movers and vendors
- Storage unit sort-outs — what stays, what sells, what goes, what stops costing you money
- Routing the outflow: resale, donation, returns, shipping — handled, not just piled by the door
- The dozens of small attached tasks that turn a one-day project into a six-month one

What comes out of a reset doesn't become your problem. The donate pile gets donated. The sell pile goes into resale. The "return this" bag actually gets returned. That's the difference between organizing and getting it handled.

## Move Support

Movers move boxes. That was never the hard part.

The hard part is the fifteen invisible problems attached to the move: deciding what makes the truck, what gets sold before it does, where the donation run goes, who's meeting the internet installer, what happens to the things that didn't fit in the car, and which utility account is still in the old name.

I work the move from both ends — what leaves the old place, and what actually happens when it arrives at the new one. Packing decisions, vendor coordination, donation and resale routing, and the follow-through that turns "we moved" into "we live here."

## Move-In Support

The truck leaving is the halfway point, not the finish line.

Move-in support is the second half: unpacking with decisions attached, rooms set up around how you'll actually use them, the kitchen arranged so you can cook on night one, the tech connected, the empty boxes out of the house. Not a showroom version of your life — a home that works on a Tuesday.

## Decision Support

Half this work is decisions, not boxes.

Keep or sell. Ship or store. Donate or return. Ask your sister or stop waiting on your sister. The piles in most homes aren't disorganization — they're postponed decisions, and they're postponed because deciding alone is exhausting.

I don't decide for you, and I don't push past grief, nostalgia, or indecision. I narrow the choices, give you honest resale numbers when "but it's worth something" comes up, keep the momentum going when a box turns out to have memories in it, and make every decision immediately real — decided things leave, get listed, or get put away the same day. Decisions that don't go anywhere just become new piles.

Between visits I'm routing the resale, scheduling the donation pickup, coordinating the vendor, and keeping the project moving. You get one person and one thread instead of four strangers and four invoices. When you text "what's happening with the garage," there's one number that knows.

# PAGE: LEGACY INVENTORY & CATALOGING

## Headline

**Legacy Inventory & Cataloging.**

Know what you have, while you can still ask what it means.

## Opening

Every family has the room.

The storage unit nobody opens. The china cabinet everyone walks past. The garage shelf of carefully saved boxes that meant something to someone you love — and nobody's entirely sure what.

Most families don't find out what they have until it's too late to ask. Then the questions arrive all at once, usually during the hardest week, usually with a deadline: what is this, who made it, what's it worth, who should have it, and why are there nine boxes labeled "MISC."

Legacy work is doing that before the deadline. Room by room, object by object — what it is, where it came from, what it's worth, and what should happen to it. Sometimes that's proactive, with the person whose story it is sitting right there telling it. Sometimes it's urgent, after. Both are this work. People move through memories at their own pace, and the pace is part of the job.

## Recognition

You'll know if this is you:

The storage unit everyone avoids opening.

The room everyone keeps avoiding because sorting through it means having conversations no one really wants to have.

The house everyone walks through one last time because every room still feels full of memories.

The collections, backup supplies, and "useful someday" items that look irrational to outsiders but make perfect sense once someone takes the time to understand the household around them.

The thing everyone walks past every day because nobody wants to be the person who starts it.

## Who This Is For

The person downsizing a family home and discovering it takes four hundred decisions, not forty.

The adult children who inherited a house in another state, a storage unit nobody asked for, and a sibling group chat that's gone quiet.

The parents who want to do this *now*, on purpose, with their own commentary attached — so the silver tea set comes with the story, not just the tarnish.

The collector who knows exactly what they have and needs it documented like it matters. Because it does.

And in this town: the stylists, studios, and creative professionals whose archive — wardrobe, props, production overflow — quietly became an unmanaged asset.

## What This Covers

- Photographic inventory — room by room, object by object, organized so a human can actually use it
- Provenance and story documentation: who made it, where it came from, what your mother said about it
- Valuation routing and appraisal coordination — knowing which items deserve a certified appraiser, and getting them one
- Distribution planning: what goes to whom, written down, while it's still a conversation and not a dispute
- Archival organization — records, photos, warranties, home documents, the filing cabinet of a whole life
- Fragile item routing and preservation decisions
- Resale routing for what the family doesn't keep — fed directly into the resale pillar, handled, not hauled
- Downsizing and home transition support, paced to the people in it

## How It Works

Legacy work starts with a walkthrough — always.

We go through the space together and figure out what's actually here: how many rooms, how much depth, what's fragile, what's valuable, what's emotional, what's just volume. Then I scope it honestly. A focused storage unit is a different project than a four-bedroom family home, and pretending otherwise is how this work goes wrong.

Then it's methodical: photograph, document, ask, record, route. The questions are half the inventory. "What's this?" has turned up war medals, a forgotten Cartier, and at least one biscuit tin of cash. It has also turned up stories nobody had heard, told because somebody finally asked while holding the object.

You end up with an inventory the whole family can see — which, for many families, ends the argument before it starts. The thing about "who gets the clock" fights is that they're rarely about the clock. A clear list, with values and stories attached, takes the fog out of it.

# PAGE: RESALE & CONSIGNMENT

## Headline

**Resale & Consignment.**

The bags leave. The money comes back.

## Opening

You know the bags.

The ones by the door, in the trunk, in the hallway. The "sell this eventually" pile that's been following you around for two years. You already did the hard part — you decided. And then the pile just… stayed, because selling things is a part-time job, and you already have at least one of those.

That's the gap this fills. I pick up, evaluate, prep, photograph, list, answer the buyers, ship the boxes, and send you the money. Across 10+ platforms, matched by what each item actually is — because the place that sells a designer bag is not the place that sells a mid-century lamp, and listing everything on one app is how things sit for a year.

This pillar also flows out of everything else. A reset surfaces what you're done with. A legacy inventory finds the things worth real money. A move closeout routes what didn't make the truck. Wherever the pile comes from, it ends up here — and you never have to become a part-time seller.

## Recognition

You'll know if this is you:

The bags that have been following you around for two years.

The designer pieces you'll "list eventually" on the app you downloaded in 2023.

The storage unit that costs more per month than its contents have earned. Ever.

The furniture from the move that doesn't fit the new place and currently lives in the garage.

The closet that no longer fits your life.

## Who This Is For

The person whose closet outgrew their life — or whose life outgrew their closet.

The family mid-downsize with a houseful of furniture and zero interest in meeting marketplace strangers in parking lots.

The person who inherited beautiful things they have no room for, and feels guilty about every option except this one.

The collector or creative professional with real inventory — designer, vintage, props, wardrobe — who needs it sold properly, not dumped.

## How It Works

1. **You ask.** Every job starts with a quick intake request — what you have, roughly how much of it. No booking fee, no pickup fee.
2. **The bags leave.** Route pickup, personal pickup, Uber courier, a prepaid shipping label, or a UPS Access Point drop — whatever's cheapest and easiest for the volume. I generally provide bags (60 or 90 quart, depending on what's coming).
3. **I prep what's worth prepping.** Steaming, lint rolling, polish, spot treatment, minor repairs, stabilizing vintage — reasonable prep when it meaningfully improves what an item sells for.
4. **Everything gets matched to its market.** 10+ platforms, chosen per item. Designer goes where designer buyers are. Furniture goes where furniture moves.
5. **I handle the buyers.** Questions, offers, hagglers, the person who asks for measurements twice and vanishes. You hear none of it.
6. **You get paid.** Commission comes out of the net sale price — after platform fees, shipping, and approved transaction costs. Payouts go out monthly, with a report of what sold and for what.

## The Quick Resale Pickup

The hands-off entry point: fill the bags, and they're gone — from your hallway, porch, or trunk. Commission-based, no fees, nothing owed up front. Start generating money or getting clothes to those in need. The fastest possible distance between "I should sell this" and "it's handled."

Two-bag limit per quick pickup (it has to fit in an Uber). More than that, tell me in the intake and we'll route it properly.

## What I Take

Clothing and accessories. Designer and luxury. Vintage with a story. Furniture and significant home goods — a major part of this work, often alongside a move, a downsize, or a legacy project. Art, decor, and the genuinely interesting.

Furniture work can include staging guidance, photography, buyer coordination, pickup logistics, and short-term operational holding during an active transition — holding, not storage; this isn't a warehouse.

Honest note: bags of mostly low-value items sometimes cost more in labor than they earn. When that's the case, I'll tell you at intake — before anything is listed — and we'll either adjust the split or reroute to donation, your call.

## Timelines & The 180-Day Review

Once your items arrive, intake review takes 7–10 business days — you'll get confirmation of what was received, what's accepted for sale, what may need hold timing, and what doesn't justify resale.

Then it's a long game, not a fast-cash service. Most strong items sell within the first 90 days; nearly everything with real demand finds its buyer within 6 to 9 months. The rhythm: 30 days, performance review. 60 days, pricing adjustment if needed. 90 days, route and markdown review. 180 days, standard term completion — anything unsold gets a decision: keep listing, reroute, return it to you, or donate it. Your preference, asked before anything moves.

Resale rewards patience, not urgency. If immediate cash is the priority, resale may not be the best route — and I'll say so.

Items are pre-owned, estate, or vintage, and are sold honestly as such — wear consistent with category, described plainly, in good faith on both sides.

# FAQ

**What exactly do you do?**
The practical realities of living — home organization, move support, house calls, legacy inventory, resale, and the dozens of details that don't fit neatly into one service. If your question starts with "is there a person who…," the answer is usually me.

**Do I have to be home?**
No, and most clients aren't. A lot of this work runs on a key handoff, photo updates, and quick text approvals. Tell me keep, sell, or donate from your phone, and it's handled by the time you're back.

**Where do you work?**
LA base, but not radius-limited. Travel runs on project logic — route-based efficiency, stops built into the flow. Quick asks can often be absorbed into an existing route, and inherited homes or family projects outside LA are scoped with travel folded in.

**What don't you do?**
Junk hauling, biohazard cleanup, hoarding remediation, heavy moving crews, legal estate administration, caregiving, medical anything, and family arbitration. I coordinate licensed professionals when they're needed — and I'm honest about when they're needed.

**Is this organizing?**
It includes organizing, but the job doesn't stop at tidy. The donate pile gets donated, the sell pile gets sold, the return gets returned, the vendor gets coordinated. The category is "handled," not "arranged."

**How does resale payment work?**
Commission on the net sale price — after platform fees and shipping — at the splits on the pricing page. Payouts go out monthly with a report. Pickup is free. An agreement covers everything before it lists.

**What if my stuff doesn't sell?**
Standard term is 180 days. At the review, unsold items get a decision — keep listing, return, reroute, or donate. You're asked before anything moves.

**Can you help my parents?**
Yes — and without making it a whole thing. House Calls were practically built for this: tech, errands, small fixes, a patient capable person who treats them like adults. If you're out of town, you get photo updates and a straight report from someone who was actually in the house.

**How do Flex Blocks work?**
Reserved 2, 4, or 6-hour work blocks — time already held for when life needs it. Best for task lists that build over time, vendor access days, donation routing, guest prep, move follow-through, and the "I need a 4-hour you" kind of month.

**What's a walkthrough, and when do I need one?**
For larger projects — whole homes, big moves, legacy work — we walk the space together first, and I scope from what's actually there. Smaller projects skip it; legacy work always starts with it.

**Why is some work $150/hr and some $175/hr?**
Organization and move work runs $150/hr with a three-hour minimum. House Calls and legacy work run $175/hr with a two-hour minimum — they carry more coordination, more judgment calls, and more between-visit work per hour on site.

**How fast can you come?**
Often fast. Same-day gets prioritized when the schedule allows, and the Four Hour Reset and Two-Hour House Call are built to book quickly. Rush couriers or special logistics, where needed, are passed through at cost.

**What's the first step?**
The initial call is complimentary. Tell me what's going on, and I'll tell you honestly what makes the most sense — including when the answer is "that's a two-hour fix, not a project."
