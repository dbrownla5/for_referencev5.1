## The name — what "The Well Lived Citizen" actually means
Get your life handled **operationally** so you get your **time** back — so you can actually **live well**.
The operations were never the point. They're the path. *"Not just your stuff — you."*

## Origin instinct — the scarves (childhood)
As a kid, Dayna came to Seattle for the music experience (EMP / MoPOP) and burned rolls of film on original cameras — through the glass — just for **Steven Tyler's scarves**, because they made her feel *part of the music.*
The object was never the point. The object is the doorway; the life behind it is the room. Same instinct, thirty years later, runs the whole company.

## The resale thesis — the fur coat (The Well Lived Closet)
A husband bought his wife a custom fur every year from a fur factory in Portland, OR. Her initials embroidered inside. The interior stitching is coming loose now. What Dayna loves is *knowing that* — wearing a marriage, not a garment. Completely different from a vintage 1920s fur off Poshmark.
→ The Well Lived Closet sells **story and provenance — not condition-and-measurements.** Where the object came from, the life it held. Nobody else in resale is selling *that.*

## "It's the person" — the House Calls thesis
- **Gail:** two hours on the phone because her card was stolen; now she can't get Netflix/Hulu back on the TV Dayna set up two years ago, and can't use the remote without Dayna there.
- **Dayna's parents** — smart, competent people who go stand at the Genius Bar for every new iPad — lost their email domain in an account change and were left hanging until they paid $500 to a stranger to migrate emails.
→ Competent people still get left hanging. *"Competence does not eliminate the need to be cared for."*

## The market thesis — the gap nobody is covering
A huge generational shift is coming with inheritance and the wealth/vintage transfer. Everyone is racing to handle the **vintage** and the **inheritance transfer** — the *stuff.* **Nobody is handling the people about to be hit by it.** That gap is the business.

## The lineage — customers need to see it
**The Well *Dressed* Citizen** = Dayna's independent freelance styling company. Men's suiting and fashion since she was young; Nordstrom (the customer-experience ethos Nordstrom *used to* build). Fashion → resale → selling → built into executive operations where she ran companies and spotted the risk → builds teams that genuinely care about the customer experience.
→ **The Well *Lived* Citizen** is the grown-up version of that same eye. The fashion/styling heritage is where the resale **authority** comes from. The thread Well Dressed → Well Lived should be visible on the site. Backstory creates credibility; it is not the pitch.

## Business architecture — owner's truth
- **Two income engines:** (1) **House Calls**, (2) **Resale / quick-sell pickups.** Quick-sell = immediate income; resale is the one she can run on her own clock — pick up, list, post, sell on her schedule — taking bigger jobs only when she has the luxury.
- **The business is fluid and tiered:** quick bag pickup → house call → downsizing → legacy catalog. Each tier flows into the next.
- **Do NOT shrink the homepage** to "moves, resale, transitions." That scales down the work, the voice, and the person. The public face holds the whole fluid business. The two-engine point is about *income and schedule*, not the public site.
