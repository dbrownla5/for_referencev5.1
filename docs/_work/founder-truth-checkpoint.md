# Founder-Truth Checkpoint

Purpose: make the business visible before more repo merging, copy cleanup, or tool rebuilding.

This checkpoint is not a repo ranking. It is the current best read of the founder's actual business model based on the strongest available evidence in this workspace.

## The business spine

1. **One interactive intake form** is the front door.
2. **Every path creates or updates one client record** in the CRM.
3. **The dashboard is the business cockpit**: clients, notes, tickets, money, calendar, documents, messages.
4. **Each service path starts its own workflow** from the same intake.
5. **Resale is one lane inside the larger business**, not the whole company.
6. **Only the resale lane opens the full chain-of-custody handshake ticket.**
7. **Agreements, templates, uploads, communications, and payouts attach back to the client record.**
8. **Copy must describe the real operating model** in Dayna's voice, not replace it with inflated AI-marketing language.

## What appears most true right now

### Founder truth about the business
- The company is a **service business with web-based client tools**, not a product business.
- The business has **two income engines**: House Calls and Resale / quick-sell pickups.
- The service ladder is fluid: quick bag pickup can lead to house call, downsizing, or legacy inventory work.
- The intake, CRM, dashboard, portal, and resale workflow are parts of **one operating system**.

### Founder truth about the customer experience
- The client should not have to figure out where they belong before reaching out.
- The intake should branch for them.
- The client record should stay intact across booking, resale, follow-up, documents, and future work.
- The dashboard exists so Dayna can run the business from one place instead of stitching together separate tools.

### Founder truth about the copy
- The public-facing promise is relief, momentum, and competent help.
- The language should sound operationally real, plain, and specific.
- Repeated polished wording is not proof of truth; it can be drift.
- Copy only counts if it reflects the actual workflow and business structure underneath it.

## What is already strongest in the current workspace

### Built and aligned
- **Handshake workflow**: intake gate, step engine, persistence, dashboard seam, consent path, payout logic.
- **Contact / intake capture seam**: current route and schema prove lead capture and persistence are already partially real.
- **Brand constants file**: one canonical code location for service facts and pricing inputs.
- **Voice audit tooling**: built mechanism for catching wording drift.

### Half-built but aligned
- **Unified CRM vision**: clearly specified, partially represented by `contact_submissions`, `handshakes`, and dashboard work, but not yet unified into one client model.
- **Dashboard expansion path**: existing handshake dashboard is the right operator surface, but still too narrow for the full business.
- **Agreement/document flow**: intent and requirements are well specified, but the full client-profile document system is not yet closed.
- **Client portal**: clearly defined, but not yet assembled as a complete self-serve surface.

### Planned and aligned
- One intake form with branching service logic.
- Unified client profile with dedupe, notes, history, follow-ups, documents, and linked resale tickets.
- Provider-agnostic payment flow with Zelle now and Stripe later.
- Messaging, uploads, and agreement status management inside the dashboard and portal.

## What is causing drift

- Treating the most finished-looking repo or page as if it were the truth.
- Letting copy cleanup blur the larger business architecture.
- Treating the handshake as the whole business instead of one lane.
- Mixing collection, judgment, and consolidation in the same artifact.
- Preserving repeated wording because it is repeated, even when it sounds unlike Dayna or weakens the operating model.

## The decision rule for the next extraction passes

Keep a fragment only if it helps preserve this chain:

**intake → client record → service workflow → dashboard visibility → client-facing follow-through**

If a fragment breaks that chain, narrows the business incorrectly, or replaces the real model with airy seminar language, it is drift even if it looks polished.
